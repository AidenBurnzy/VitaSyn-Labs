Reconstruction Workflow

Peptide Reconstruction & Handling

Achieve consistent experimental performance by pairing clean technique with precise measurements. The protocol below outlines the reconstruction path from vial inspection through aliquoting and storage.

Preparation & Setup

Stage all supplies prior to breaking the vial seal to limit exposure to ambient humidity.

  • Allow lyophilized vials to reach room temperature while sealed.
  • Prepare sterile work surface under a Class II biosafety cabinet or laminar flow hood.
  • Assemble sterile syringes, low-protein-binding filters, and pre-chilled aliquot tubes.

Diluent Selection

Match the diluent to the sequence. Utilize bacteriostatic water or sterile saline unless CoA guidance specifies otherwise.

  • Warm diluent to 18–22 °C to reduce foaming during addition.
  • Adjust pH within the recommended stability window for the peptide.
  • Always record lot numbers for diluent and filters used.

Sterility Controls

Mitigate contamination by enforcing aseptic touchpoints.

  • Sanitize gloves with 70% IPA between each vial interaction.
  • Inspect the stopper for particulates after piercing; discard if compromised.
  • Employ single-use needles and discard them into appropriate sharps containers immediately.

Step-by-Step Protocol

  1. Open the vial and introduce the first 10–20% of the diluent slowly along the glass wall to dampen the cake.
  2. Gently swirl—never shake—until the powder dissolves fully. Avoid creating bubbles or foam.
  3. Complete the volume with remaining diluent while maintaining laminar flow coverage.
  4. Withdraw solution through a sterile 0.22 µm filter into labeled aliquot tubes sized for single-use experiments.
  5. Document the final concentration, diluent, and expiration date in the lab notebook and digital inventory system.
Tip If solubility issues appear, consult the peptide datasheet for compatible co-solvents (such as acetic acid or DMSO). Introduce them incrementally <5% v/v while monitoring clarity.

Post-Mix Handling

Aliquoted solutions degrade faster than lyophilized material. Build a rotation that matches experimental cadence to minimize waste.

Immediate Use

Store aliquots destined for same-day work on wet ice and discard leftovers after the session concludes.

7-Day Window

Refrigerate at 2–8 °C and schedule potency checks if the peptide is sensitive to hydrolysis.

Long-Term

Snap-freeze at -20 °C or below. Avoid repeated freeze-thaw cycles—create enough aliquots to keep each vial single-use.

Quality Assurance

A lightweight QA review after every reconstruction maintains confidence in downstream assays.

  • Retain a 0.5 mL archive aliquot at -80 °C for re-testing if questions arise.
  • Capture conductivity, pH, and clarity observations in the batch record.
  • Schedule periodic microbial screening for frequently used peptides.
