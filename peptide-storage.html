Storage Protocols

Peptide Storage Best Practices

Preserve peptide integrity by pairing precise temperature control with thoughtful handling habits. The guidelines below summarize the procedures our lab team relies on when maintaining research-grade materials.

-20 °C & Below

Most lyophilized peptides remain stable for 12–24 months when stored in a frost-free, temperature-logged freezer.

  • House vials in airtight secondary containers with desiccant packs.
  • Log freezer openings and defrost cycles to flag excursions quickly.
  • Allow vials to equilibrate to room temperature before breaking the vacuum seal to avoid condensation.

2–8 °C Refrigeration

Short-term storage for peptides prepared for upcoming experiments.

  • Restrict residence time to <30 days for lyophilized lots and <7 days for reconstituted aliquots.
  • Use light-protective sleeves when the sequence contains photolabile residues.
  • Label vials with prep date, concentration, and technician initials.

Ambient Transit

When shipping or staging at room temperature, reduce time out of controlled storage.

  • Employ insulated shippers with cold packs for any transit exceeding four hours.
  • Maintain chain-of-custody documentation that records hand-offs and arrival temperatures.
  • Inspect vials upon receipt and return to cold storage immediately.

Controlled Handling Routine

Schedule peptide retrievals to coincide with experimental prep windows. Minimize thermal cycling by pre-labeling racks, laying out dry ice, and staging diluents ahead of time. Every removal from cold storage should have a documented rationale.

Review storage log for recent excursions before opening the freezer.
Wear powder-free gloves and change them after handling shipping materials.
Wipe exterior vials with 70% IPA to remove frost and particulates prior to use.
Return unused vials within 2 minutes to avoid unnecessary thaw cycles.
Stability Reminder Continuous monitoring is essential. Integrate storage units with a calibrated temperature probe and configure SMS/email alerts for deviations beyond ±2 °C.

Stability Planning Timeline

Use the following timeline as a planning reference when working with newly received peptide lots. Always defer to certificate of analysis (CoA) data for sequence-specific limits.

Day 0–3

Verify shipment temperature logs, inspect vial seals, and enter each lot into the asset registry. Photograph labels for digital backups.

Week 1

Schedule baseline potency or purity checks for critical sequences. Record aliquot map for any subdivided vials.

Month 3

Audit freezer inventory. Discard or re-test items that have reached their recommended re-evaluation interval.

Documentation Essentials

Traceability protects both your data and downstream collaborators. Maintain a digital storage log that pairs lot numbers with container positions and environmental data.

  • Capture electronic signatures for each storage event and corrective action.
  • Attach supporting documents such as CoAs, stability reports, and deviation notes.
  • Review logs weekly to confirm inventory counts align with experimental consumption.
